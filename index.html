"The SSB Problem"

For many years, Trondheim Municipality depended on yearly reports from Statistics Norway (SSB). Because of uncertainties regarding the quality of this data, SSB decided to discontinue these yearly reports in 2012.1

Trondheim Municipality is therefore acting somewhat in the dark when trying to figure out how to reach the goal of reducing the municipality's GHG emission levels by 25% by 2020 and 70-90% by 2050 (compared to 1991 levels).2

The Project

The Carbon Track & Trace (CTT) project will develop, together with the City of Trondheim (Norway), an automated GHG emissions inventory software system. The CTT project is a partnership between Trondheim Municipality, Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) and ICLEI, and will contribute to mapping out emissions in the building and transport sectors.

This timely project comes back to back with local decision makers looking for more accurate ways to measure the impact of Trondheim’s mitigation strategies.
